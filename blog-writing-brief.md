# Blue Tape Sites blog writing brief

Blue Tape Sites writes for **plumbers, electricians, cleaners, contractors, and owner-operators** who want a better website, stronger local visibility, and more believable proof online. The tone should feel **specific, practical, lightly opinionated, and human**, not corporate, inflated, or robotic.

## Voice rules

Write like a sharp consultant who has actually reviewed a lot of weak service-business websites. Use full paragraphs, clear transitions, and occasional light wit, but stay grounded. Avoid buzzwords, obvious filler, exaggerated certainty, and anything that sounds like a generic AI content template.

## Compliance rules

Do **not** write with the goal of hiding authorship or evading detectors. The goal is simply to make the writing natural, readable, and useful. Do not mention AI detectors, evasion, or manipulation. Just write well.

## Article structure

Each article should:

1. Open with a practical hook tied to the reader's real situation.
2. Explain the core issue in plain language.
3. Give concrete guidance with examples from home-service businesses.
4. Include at least 3 Markdown `##` section headings.
5. Include one short bullet list only when it clarifies a process or checklist.
6. End with a brief conclusion that ties the topic back to trust, leads, or clearer positioning.

## Style preferences

Favor short, declarative sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones. Use examples involving plumbers, electricians, cleaners, or contractors. Avoid fake statistics, fake case studies, or claims that require sourcing. Keep each article between roughly 900 and 1400 words.

## Output format

Return Markdown only. Start with the article title as `# Title`, then write the body. Do not include YAML front matter.
