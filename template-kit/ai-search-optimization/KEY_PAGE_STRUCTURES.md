# Recommended On-Page Structures for AI Search and Lead Generation

## Purpose

These page structures are designed to help Blue Tape Sites rank in traditional search, appear in AI-generated answers, and convert that visibility into qualified inbound leads. Each page should lead with a clear answer, reinforce entity clarity, and create an obvious next step.

| Page type | H1 pattern | Opening requirement | Required support sections | Primary CTA |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Web Design for Service Businesses | Explain who the company serves and what outcome it delivers in 2-3 sentences | Proof, fit, service explanation, FAQs, audit block, internal links to trade and blog pages | Free audit |
| Trade page | Web Design for Plumbers / Electricians / Cleaners | State the trade, the common website problem, and the business outcome | Trade-specific mistakes, trust blockers, what a strong site needs, proof, FAQs | Free audit |
| City page | Website Design for Service Businesses in [City] | State why local trust and local relevance matter in that market | Local proof, market context, FAQs, internal links to relevant trade content | Free audit |
| Blog post | Specific search-intent question | Give a direct answer immediately under the H1 | Step-by-step guidance, examples, FAQs, related links, contextual CTA | Audit or contact |
| Proof page | Website Audit Examples / Before-and-After Reviews | Show what changed and why it matters | Screenshots, critique, lessons, service fit, CTA | Free audit |

## Homepage blueprint

The homepage should begin with a precise H1 that names the audience and desired outcome. The first paragraph should immediately define Blue Tape Sites as a web design partner for home-service businesses and explain that the result is more trust and more inbound leads. Below that, add a short proof section, then a section explaining what makes service-business websites convert, then a trade-fit section, then FAQs, and finally the audit CTA.

```txt
H1: Web Design for Home-Service Businesses That Need More Leads
Subhead: Blue Tape Sites builds lead-focused websites for plumbers, electricians, cleaners, and contractors.
Section 1: Why most service-business websites lose trust and leads
Section 2: What Blue Tape Sites improves
Section 3: Who it is for
Section 4: Proof / examples / audit screenshots
Section 5: FAQs
Section 6: Free audit CTA
Internal links: plumber page, electrician page, cleaner page, blog, proof page
```

## Trade page blueprint

Trade pages should never be generic duplicates. Each one should use the actual language, buying triggers, and trust concerns of that trade. A plumber page should mention emergency trust, booking urgency, and credibility. An electrician page should emphasize legitimacy, safety, and professionalism. A cleaner page should emphasize trust, professionalism, recurring work, and presentation.

```txt
H1: Web Design for Plumbers
Opening answer: If your plumbing website looks generic, slow, or hard to trust, it will cost you estimate requests and service calls.
Section 1: What plumbing customers look for on a website
Section 2: Why many plumber websites fail to convert
Section 3: What a high-performing plumbing homepage should include
Section 4: Why Blue Tape Sites is a fit for plumbers
Section 5: FAQs
Section 6: Free audit CTA
```

## City page blueprint

City pages should exist only for real target markets. They should be specific, not spun or templated junk. Use the city page to reinforce geographic familiarity, local-trust expectations, and the kinds of service businesses likely to operate there.

```txt
H1: Website Design for Service Businesses in Orange County
Opening answer: Service businesses in Orange County need websites that look credible fast, explain their offer clearly, and turn local search traffic into booked work.
Section 1: What matters in this market
Section 2: Common local website trust problems
Section 3: How Blue Tape Sites helps businesses in this area
Section 4: Related trade links
Section 5: FAQs
Section 6: Free audit CTA
```

## Blog post blueprint

Blog posts should be written for answer extraction first and expansion second. Put the clearest answer in the first paragraph. Then move into examples, mistakes, checklists, and a CTA tied to the reader’s likely situation.

```txt
H1: What Makes a Contractor Website Convert?
Direct answer paragraph
Section 1: The five elements that matter most
Section 2: Mistakes that kill trust
Section 3: How to improve your homepage
Section 4: When to ask for help
Section 5: FAQ
CTA: Want this applied to your site? Request a free audit.
```

## Conversion-page requirements

Every key page should include one primary CTA, visible proof near the CTA, and a low-friction explanation of what happens after submission. Keep forms short. Avoid vague agency copy. Speak directly to the prospect, their problem, and the next step.
