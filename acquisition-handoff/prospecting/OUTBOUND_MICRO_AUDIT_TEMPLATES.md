# Outbound Micro-Audit Templates

**Purpose:** Provide ready-to-use outbound messages for the first Blue Tape Sites prospecting sprint.

## How These Templates Should Be Used

These templates are not meant for mass blasting. Each one should be customized with one or two real observations pulled from the prospect’s website. The value of Blue Tape Sites outreach comes from specificity. If the message could have been sent to any contractor, it is not ready.

The strongest messages do three things at once. They show that Blue Tape Sites noticed something real, they connect that issue to trust or conversion, and they make the next step feel easy.

## Template 1: Standard Email

> **Subject:** Quick website note for [Company Name]  
> Hi [First Name], I took a quick look at your website and noticed a couple of things that may be costing you trust before people ever call. The business looks real, but the site is not fully carrying the same confidence yet. In particular, [specific issue] and [specific issue] stood out. I help contractors tighten those exact gaps so the site feels as credible as the work itself. If helpful, I can send a short three-point teardown showing what I’d fix first.

## Template 2: Plumber Version

> **Subject:** Quick website note for [Company Name]  
> Hi [First Name], I looked at your site and noticed a couple of things that may be slowing down trust on mobile when someone is ready to call fast. In particular, [specific issue] and [specific issue] stood out. For plumbing companies, those small moments can create hesitation right when the lead should be moving. If useful, I can send a short teardown with the first few fixes I’d make.

## Template 3: Remodeler Version

> **Subject:** Quick website note for [Company Name]  
> Hi [First Name], I took a quick look at your website and the main thing that stood out is that the quality of the work may not be coming through as strongly online as it likely does in person. In particular, [specific issue] and [specific issue] stood out. I help remodelers tighten presentation, trust, and project-storytelling so the site pre-sells the work better before the first consultation. If helpful, I can send a short teardown with what I’d improve first.

## Template 4: Follow-Up After No Reply

> Hi [First Name], just following up in case this is useful. The main reason I reached out is that your site appears to have one or two fixable gaps around [message angle], and those are often the kinds of issues that quietly cost trust before a lead ever contacts you. Happy to leave it there if the timing is not right, but glad to send the short teardown if it would help.

## Template 5: Final Light Close-Out

> Hi [First Name], closing the loop on this. I reached out because the website looked like it might be underselling the business a bit, especially around [message angle]. No pressure at all if it is not a priority right now. If it becomes one later, I’d be glad to take another look.

## Short DM Version

> Quick note — I looked at your site and noticed a couple of trust/conversion gaps around [message angle]. The business looks solid, but the site may be underselling it a bit. If useful, I can send a short teardown with the first few fixes I’d make.

## Message-Angle Prompts

| Angle | When to Use It |
|---|---|
| **Mobile trust** | The site feels cramped, dated, or unclear on a phone |
| **CTA clarity** | The next step is buried or confusing |
| **Proof** | The business lacks enough visible reasons to believe |
| **Premium presentation** | The work appears stronger than the site’s design and structure |
| **Qualification** | The site is likely attracting weaker or less prepared inquiries |

## Customization Checklist

Before sending, the next agent should confirm all of the following:

| Check | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Specificity** | The message includes at least one real observation |
| **Fit** | The company genuinely matches the current sprint niches |
| **Tone** | The message sounds helpful, not superior or spammy |
| **Length** | The message stays short and easy to scan |
| **Next step** | The CTA is simple: send a short teardown or take a brief call |

## Final Reminder

The best Blue Tape Sites outbound does not try to win the whole sale by email. It tries to win the right to continue the conversation.
