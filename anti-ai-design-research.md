# Anti-AI Design Research Notes

The first pattern repeated across recent design commentary is that AI-built websites tend to converge on the **statistical average** of the modern web. In practice, that means safe sans-serif typography, generic left-text/right-visual hero structures, repeated rounded cards, soft shadows, light gradients, and predictable three-card section layouts. These patterns are not inherently bad, but when stacked together without strong brand-specific editing they create a "seen this before" reaction rather than a credible, crafted impression.

A second pattern is that these sites often feel emotionally flat even when they are technically clean. The design can look polished at first glance while still feeling low-effort because the hierarchy is too familiar, the content blocks are too evenly distributed, and the visual language does not appear tied to a real business personality. The result is sameness: interchangeable pages, weak distinction, and a sense that the site was assembled from common defaults instead of intentionally designed.

For Blue Tape Sites, the redesign should move away from anything that reads as default startup UI. The visual direction should favor more deliberate editorial restraint, fewer repetitive pill-and-card motifs, stronger typographic hierarchy, more selective use of accent color, and more business-like proof framing. The site should feel less like a generated landing page and more like a professional studio or consultancy website built with confidence and taste.

## Current Blue Tape Sites diagnosis

The current homepage already has a strong concept, but several details still push it toward the familiar AI-builder look. The most obvious are the repeated **pill shapes**, the blue gradient CTA treatment, the oversized soft-shadow card framing in the hero, and the repeated card-grid rhythm that makes several sections feel assembled from the same component family. The current palette and component styling are consistent, but they are a little too smooth and universal, which lowers the sense of authorship.

The hero also still carries some generated-art energy because the composition combines a conceptual tape illustration, floating rounded frames, and glossy overlays in a way that feels more like an AI mockup than a real design studio website. The typography itself is strong, but the surrounding presentation is too polished in a default way rather than in a businesslike, editorial, or distinctly branded way.

The next redesign should therefore remove or reduce the signals of sameness. That means fewer decorative pills, flatter and more confident surfaces, more restrained shadows, more asymmetry, more disciplined spacing, more text-led proof, and more "real studio" cues instead of conceptual interface theater. Blue Tape Sites should feel more like a sharp consultancy with taste and less like a generated premium landing page.

## Practical redesign direction

| Keep | Change |
| --- | --- |
| Strong headline hierarchy | Reduce repeated pill badges and rounded chip motifs |
| Clear service-business positioning | Replace glossy gradient CTA look with more restrained button styling |
| Blue tape brand metaphor | Make tape accents feel precise and selective instead of decorative everywhere |
| Clean mobile structure | Introduce more editorial spacing and fewer repeated cards |
| Strong CTA clarity | Swap conceptual mockup energy for more credible proof and studio presentation |
